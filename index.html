How To Prepare For An Effective Interview

Preparation Time: 30 minutes to 1 hour
Awesome rating: 20% awesome

Preparing for an interview seems like a daunting task. It doesn't have to be. Follow this guide!

  • Things You'll Need

  • A web browser
  • A notebook
  • A pen or pencil
  • Imagination

  1. Do your research on the person.

    Before you can write out good questions to ask someone who you are interviewing for an article, podcast, or another medium, you need to gather all the information you can. Write down a bio of your person to help nail down specific topics.

  2. Write down your objective for the interview.

    Once you know who this person you’re interviewing is, it’s a good idea to write down what you want from the interview. Your objective should be a declarative statement which is concise.

  3. Lead with a “softball question”.

    A “softball question” will help the interviewee become relaxed and open up.

  4. Ask open-ended questions.

    Whether you’re reporting or doing an informational interview with someone who works somewhere you want to. You want to create a dialogue, this means questions that don’t let the interviewee simply answer “yes” or “no” will help you.

  5. Ask questions that elicit an emotional response.

    This will humanize your subject to your readers and/or viewers by demonstrating what makes them passionate.
